Rachel & DJ here (but mostly Rachel). Thank you Tanner for letting us host the last 4TF of 2020! We’ll keep this short and sweet unlike this disaster of a year.

Indie Christmas | Joel The Lump of Coal | The Killers

So I really don't like Christmas music, but since it is Christmas day I am obligated to share at least one. Joel The Lump of Coal is a non traditional Christmas song by the very well known band The Killers. I like it because the song is personifying a lump of coal, Jimmy Kimmel is the voice of Santa, and that's it.

Electronic Indie | Get to Know You | Tomo Nakayama

Moving on from the holiday jingles, we have a Seattle native, Tomo Nakayama. Tomo’s music is chill, upbeat, and has super melodic tones. Some of his music gives me strong Elliot Smith vibes, like "Miss Misery." I found this song via my discover weekly and let me just say: *in to it*.

Electronic Indie | Calling out | IS TROPICAL

Another song on my discover weekly from a few weeks ago, "Calling out" is IS TROPICAL's first song since 2016. Similar to Tomo, Is Tropical has fun punchy indie vibes with their music and it's a lot of what I’ve been listening to this year apart from Harry Styles. The first time I heard this band was when Kaitlin played their V interesting music video for the song "The Greeks."

Film Score Heroic R&B | Survivor | 2WEI

In honor of making it to the almost-end of 2020, this song is dedicated to all of us. We survived! Ok, besides that, this cover is incredible and DJ’s contribution to 4TF this week.

(DJ now...) A slow and epic cover of my favorite Destiny’s Child song, done by a duo out of Hamburg, Germany. I hope this song will inspire everyone as they begin to write their new year's resolutions and as we look forward to a year that hopefully isn’t as much of a dumpster fire as 2020.
