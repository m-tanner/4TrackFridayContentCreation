Poetry | Woozy With Cider | James Yorkston (feat. Jon Hopkins)

Lyrics

Shawn sent me this and, though it was just outside of my musical comfort zone, it hit me in a place that usually resonates more with slam poetry (see 4TF's previous shared "Tiny Glowing Screens, Pt. 2").

Best experienced while reading along...

James Yorkston delivers a poem in his beautiful Scottish voice, softly handling the ups and downs of life and the uncertainty that comes with creative effort. The way he wonders if anyone's ever listened to his music by asking, "if anyone heard those words that I never got quite right." The way he continues on, wondering if he'll "end up owning the whole street" or "[sleep] in the park" instead, drills down on the doubt inside all of us... the differences between the country and the city... the loss of loved ones (to the city)... the city's anonymity... the relationship with a bartender... the lyrical cadence is the best part

Nu Jazz | Everything Is Transformed | Hugo Kant

When Tamas shared Hugo Kant with me last week, I knew I'd found a new favorite multi-instrumentalist.

Kant started his solo project in France with 2011's release of Searching London. However, this comes from last year's Far from Home, an album rife with eclectic joys. One track even features samples from JFK's space-focused speeches, particularly fitting given the most recent successful NASA Perseverance landing.

Reminding me of other classics, Kant injects just enough freshness...
